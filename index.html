A legend and a mathematical problem

According to legend, the game of chess was invented 3000 years before Jesus Christ in India by Sessa.

This game became so popular that the Indian ruler at the time had Sessa called to find out about it.

Delighted by this game, the sovereign wanted to reward Sessa by letting him choose what he wanted ...

Sessa then proposed: "I want the number of grains of wheat that the number of squares of the chessboard would produce: only one for the first, 2 for the second, 4 for the third, and so on, always doubling until the sixty -fourth

The sovereign accepted, but a few days later, one of his advisers came to see him distraught: the kingdom's wheat reserves were insufficient ...
